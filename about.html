About

Somnus is the world economy, rendered as a planet.

Somnus (codename TERRA in the command center) is a browser-native financial visualization. Instead of a wall of tickers, it puts global markets where they actually happen — on a live, holographic 3D Earth you can spin, search, and drill into. It's a prototype built on mock data, structured so a live backend can slot in without touching the render code.

The data layers

Seven layers stack on the globe. To keep the planet readable, the command center renders the market heatmap plus at most two heavy layers at once.

  • Market Heatmap — 25 major indices tint their countries green→red by daily change.
  • Market Sessions — exchange beacons pulse open or closed against real time zones.
  • Oil — top producing regions sized by barrels per day.
  • Population — density heat over the world's most crowded corridors.
  • Mega-Cities — the 20 largest urban centers as light beams.
  • Metals & Mining — gold, copper, lithium, and rare-earth production zones.
  • Trade Arcs — major shipping lanes drawn as animated great-circle arcs.

Tech stack

  • Three.js (r128) — WebGL rendering: globe shader, atmosphere, merged border geometry, instanced markers.
  • Natural Earth via world-atlas TopoJSON — real country borders at 50m resolution, parsed with topojson-client.
  • Vanilla JS + CSS — no framework, no build step, no backend. Every dependency is a CDN URL.
  • Static hosting — published from a single branch to GitHub Pages; every push goes live.
